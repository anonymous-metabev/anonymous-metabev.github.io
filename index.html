MetaBEV: Solving Sensor Failures and Tasks Conflict in BEV Perception

ICCV Anonymous Submission 7004

We propose MetaBEV to sufficiently solve sensor failures (inluding large-scale sensor corruption and extreme sensor missing) and tasks conflict in BEV perception.

Abstract

Multi-sensors (e.g., camera and LiDAR) perception has shown its great superiority in autonomous driving. However, sensor failures and task conflicts lead to inaccurate recognition, comprising the autonomous safety.

In this paper, we propose \model\ to address the above two challenges. Specifically, we adopt modal-specific attention modules to flexibly process cameras, LiDAR, or both modalities for generating fused BEV representations. Meanwhile, the Multi-task Mixture-of-Experts (\moe) is integrated to alleviate the disparate optimization in multi-task learning. We evaluate \model\ on nuScenes dataset~\cite{caesar-20cvpr-nuscenes}, on which large-scale sensor corruptions and sensor missing are formulated. Experiments show that \model\ achieves state-of-the-art performance in both full/corrupted modality and multi-task learning, demonstrating its flexibility, robustness, and adaptability. For example, when encountering extreme sensor failures (e.g., missing all six cameras), \model\ achieves 69.2\% NDS on 3D detection, exhibiting significant superiority to current methods. Besides, \model\ sets a new state-of-the-art mIoU of 70.4\% on BEV map segmentation.
